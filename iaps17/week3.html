FIRST PROJECT: PRESENTATIONS

Everyone will need to give a 5-10 minute talk, detailing their plans for the first project.

This will be worth 5% of your final grade in this class and will be evaluated on its content, preparation, and delivery.

Your presentation should demonstrate that you:

  1. Are knowledgeable about the kind of work you are proposing (Do your research)
  2. Have put considerable thought into the scope of your proposed project (Plan & schedule)
  3. Already possess the majority of the skills needed to see the project through (Exercise your current skillset)

PRESENTATION CONTENT

The presentation must include:

  1. A working title, plus your name
  2. A concise and compelling description of what you want to make
  3. Examples of sketches, prototypes, or other preliminary materials which are visual or aural
  4. 1 example of work previously made by you (collaborative work ok!) which is relevant to your project
  5. 5 examples of work created by others which consider successful and are relevant to your project (artwork, sound, design, games, etc.)
  6. 5 examples of research done into the subject matter, themes, or concepts of your project (NOT artwork, sound, design, games, etc.)
  7. A brief outline of the schedule for the project which provides specific weekly goals
